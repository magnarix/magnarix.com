The MagnaRix Platform

MagnaRix is built around a single principle: a decision is a first-class object in the enterprise. It is a structured entity with state, ownership, and lineage — governed from formation through execution.

Decision Canvas

A Structured Environment for Decision Formation

The Decision Canvas is the primary workspace in MagnaRix. It provides a structured environment where each decision is composed from clearly defined elements — capturing the full context of how and why a choice is being made.

  • Objectives and intended outcomes
  • Constraints and boundary conditions
  • Assumptions held at the time of the decision
  • Alternatives considered and reasons for selection
  • Trade-offs accepted and their justification
  • Risks identified during formation

Risk & Mitigation

Structured Risk Modeling Within the Decision

Each decision in MagnaRix can carry a detailed risk model. Risks are not external annotations — they are first-class elements of the decision record, linked to specific mitigations and assigned owners.

  • Probability and impact assessment per risk
  • Mitigation strategies with responsible owners
  • Risk status tracking across the decision lifecycle
  • Audit-grade record of risk acceptance or escalation

Approval & Governance

Governed Workflows with Clear Accountability

MagnaRix supports structured approval workflows where decisions move through defined stages — from draft to submitted to reviewed to approved. Once approved, the decision record is locked, preserving the state at the time of authorization.

  • Submission with stated rationale and supporting context
  • Reviewer assignment and structured review stages
  • Approval with timestamped authorization records
  • Locked decision state preserving the approved context

Decision Memory

Institutional Continuity Through Persistent Records

Every decision in MagnaRix persists as an immutable record. This creates an institutional memory that outlasts individual contributors, team reorganizations, and leadership transitions. Teams inherit not just outcomes, but the full context of why those outcomes were chosen.

Decision memory enables cross-team learning, reduces repeated analysis, and ensures that prior reasoning remains accessible when revisiting or extending earlier choices.

Replay & Audit

Reconstruct What Was Known at the Time

MagnaRix supports decision replay — the ability to reconstruct the full context of a decision as it existed at any point in its lifecycle. This means auditors, reviewers, and future teams can see what was known, what was assumed, what risks were accepted, and who approved the decision.

Replay provides a temporal view of decision-making that supports governance reviews, compliance audits, and organizational learning.

Execution Guidance

From Decision to Operational Follow-Through

MagnaRix is expanding from decision formation into next-step guidance and operational follow-through. The goal is to connect execution directly to the decision that authorized it — so intent, constraints, and accountability carry forward into action.

Intent Preservation

Execution steps reference the original decision, its objectives, and its constraints — preventing drift between authorization and action.

Traceability

Each operational step maintains a link back to the governing decision, supporting accountability and audit requirements across the execution lifecycle.

Continuity

When execution teams change or programs evolve, the connection to the originating decision ensures that context is not lost in transition.

Architecture

Platform Architecture Summary

MagnaRix is composed of six integrated layers, each supporting a distinct aspect of the decision lifecycle.

L6 Execution Layer Next-step guidance and operational follow-through
L5 Intelligence Layer AI summaries, assumption surfacing, risk signals
L4 Memory Layer Immutable decision records and institutional continuity
L3 Governance Layer Approval workflows, ownership, and audit trail
L2 Reasoning & Risk Layer Assumptions, alternatives, constraints, risk modeling
L1 Decision Layer Structured canvas, context capture, decision composition
